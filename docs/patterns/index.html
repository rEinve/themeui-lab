Patterns

Patterns combine primitives and components into larger page-level structures. They are not core UI components.

Canonical pattern usage should stay in the pattern layer. Top bar and off-canvas navigation belong here because they manage page shell, responsive behavior, and navigation structure. Hero belongs here too because it composes page regions, content flow, and components into one landing-page section. Related posts belongs here for the same reason: it composes a section header, actions, carousel behavior, and reusable cards.

Related Posts

Use this pattern when a page needs an editorial recommendation rail with a section header, a browse action, and multi-card carousel controls.

Contract: .related-posts owns the section composition only. Keep the cards themselves in .post-card and let the carousel component own viewport movement and button wiring.

Do

Use the pattern when the page needs a cohesive recommendation section with header content and carousel controls living together.

Don't

Turn the card component into a section-level abstraction or hard-code section spacing into the carousel itself.
